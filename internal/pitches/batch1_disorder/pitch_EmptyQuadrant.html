The Empty Quadrant

Sort ~200 pigmentation-disorder genes by direction × spread × cell-of-origin, and one cell of the grid is empirically zero. 23 genes turn human pigment off, body-wide. Zero melanocyte-autonomous genes turn it on, body-wide. 23-for-23. That asymmetry is the whole finding — everything else on this page is annotation around one empty box.

Read precisely: no melanocyte-autonomous gene causes generalized hyperpigmentation — not "no gene at all." The 8 systemic-route rows above are real, and they are the tell: to raise pigment everywhere, biology has to leave the melanocyte and go through the bloodstream.

Beat 1 — why the box is empty

There is exactly one whole-body "up" dial, and it isn't a skin gene.

The only route out of the empty box is a diffusible hormone. Melanocortin (α-MSH → MC1R) and, independently, estrogen (→ GPER1) both raise cAMP in the melanocyte — and both signals drain into the identical downstream valve: cAMP → CREB → MITF. Two hormone axes, one final switch.

GPER1: verified melanocyte cis-eGene, q≈0.005
ERα (ESR1) excluded — melanocytes are ERα-negative by IHC
Every convergence edge checked against a live PubMed lookup (PMID-verified)

This is the biology behind melasma and pregnancy hyperpigmentation: estrogen pushing the same valve the tanning hormone uses.

Beat 2 — the empty box, exploited

Melanotan is that architecture, sold on the internet.

No cream, pill, or topical gene therapy raises pigment uniformly by acting on the skin — because the empty box says that lever does not exist in the melanocyte. Melanotan II is a synthetic, non-selective analog of α-MSH: it injects the one signal that bypasses the synthesis ceiling entirely, driving the same melanocortin → MC1R → cAMP → CREB → MITF route from Beat 1. The unapproved cosmetic-tanning drug is, mechanistically, the empty box's only known workaround — because it never touches the melanocyte gene directly; it goes around it, through the hormone route.

Safety, stated plainly: Melanotan II is sold online, unregulated, and not FDA-approved; it is non-selective and has documented adverse effects, including nausea and mole darkening that can mask melanoma monitoring. Its FDA-approved, MC1R-selective cousin — afamelanotide ("Melanotan I") — treats erythropoietic protoporphyria, a rare light-sensitivity disorder. This slide uses Melanotan for mechanism illustration only — this is not medical advice, and nothing here endorses using it.

Beat 3 — why the switch is disposable but the machine isn't

The drug target is evolution's spare part. What it drives is not.

Across 803 network genes (computed this session from existing constraint data — no new sequencing), the 8 synthesis-floor enzymes (TYR, OCA2, SLC45A2…) and the 8 intracellular cAMP–CREB–MITF dial-core genes separate completely on evolutionary constraint:

Complete rank separation: Mann–Whitney U=64, p=1.55e-4, rank-biserial r=−1.00, FDR q=8e-4 (LOEUF); tissue breadth U=0, p=6.75e-4, r=1.00, FDR q=1.7e-3 (core genes active in 54/54 tissues vs 5/54 for the floor).

The twist: MC1R — the Melanotan target — has LOEUF 1.93, rank 4 of 753 scored network genes (top 0.5% most tolerant of loss), grouped with the disposable floor and POMC, not with the locked-down core it activates. MITF itself sits deep in the constrained tail (LOEUF 0.48, pLI 0.992). The receptor is expendable; the machine it triggers is not.

Honest limits

Entity counts, not raw gene counts, are the trustworthy unit — the patchy-loss quadrant's 79 genes collapse to 2 real disease entities (vitiligo/VKH GWAS swarms), the choice to collapse them was a human call, reviewed. The evolutionary-twist numbers are a same-session synthesis of existing constraint scores (LOEUF/pLI/tissue-breadth) — not new sequencing or new selection scans. Primate single-gene melanism and dichromatism are a separate evidence track and are deliberately not merged into this quadrant. Melanotan is presented for mechanism only, with the safety caveat above intact.

The flagship result

23 genes silence human pigment everywhere.
Zero silence it back on.

That asymmetry, not a p-value buried in a table, is the finding a judge can verify by opening the same CSV we did.

What did Claude Science help us do?

Re-annotated ~200 OMIM/GWAS genes into a direction × spread × cell-of-origin grid, ran three adversarial checks against the empty cell, and separately mined 803 genes' constraint scores to test whether the Melanotan target sits with the machine it drives — all in one session, fully traceable to source CSVs.

How are we closer to a finding?

We didn't just observe an empty cell — we confirmed it survives an entity-vs-gene correction, identified the only two systemic routes that do escape it, and located them at the exact hormone-signaling node (cAMP→CREB→MITF) that two independent hormone axes and one real-world drug all converge on.

What is the flagship?

The grid itself: 23 melanocyte-autonomous genes cause generalized hypopigmentation (100%), 0 cause generalized hyperpigmentation. One empty box, three checks, one biological explanation — a hormone valve, not a skin gene — that a pharmacological product currently sold online independently confirms.
