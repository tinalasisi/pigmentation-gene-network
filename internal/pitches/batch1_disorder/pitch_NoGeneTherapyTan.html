Beat 1 — The verified flagship

The empty cell in the disorder table

We classified ~200 OMIM/GWAS pigmentation-disorder genes by direction (less vs. more pigment), spread (whole-body vs. patchy) and cell of origin. Restricted to genes that act inside the melanocyte itself, the whole-body / more-pigment cell is empty:

Generalized + melanocyte-autonomous → 23 genes / 6 disease entities, and every single one of them is hypopigmenting — albinism (OCA), Hermansky-Pudlak, Chediak-Higashi, Griscelli, Tietz/MITF syndrome. Zero cause generalized hyperpigmentation.

Flip to patchy spread and the same cell type does both directions freely — 28 hyperpigmenting genes vs. 7 hypopigmenting. Melanocytes are not incapable of turning color up; they just can't do it uniformly, body-wide, alone.

The only genes that DO cause generalized hyperpigmentation are not melanocyte genes at all: 6 endocrine/ACTH-excess genes (1 entity) and 2 metabolic genes, ATP7B / Wilson disease copper toxicity (1 entity) — systemic routes that reach the melanocyte from outside it. That's the exception that proves the rule, not a contradiction of it.

Beat 2 — Why the cell is empty

Two hormones, one valve — and nothing downstream of it is a light switch

The one path that raises pigment everywhere at once is diffusible, not cell-autonomous: the melanocortin hormone α-MSH binds the melanocyte receptor MC1R, raising cAMP, activating CREB, which turns on MITF — the master regulator that drives the whole melanin-synthesis program up.

A second, independent hormone arm reaches the exact same valve: estrogen, acting through the membrane receptor GPER1 (a verified melanocyte cis-eGene, q≈0.005 — not classical ERα, which melanocytes don't express), also raises cAMP and funnels into the identical cAMP → CREB → MITF node.

Two independent hormone axes, melanocortin and estrogen — one shared final valve. More than one hand on the switch, but only one switch. (This is the mechanistic backbone of melasma and pregnancy-associated hyperpigmentation.)
Beat 3 — The prediction, sold on the internet

Melanotan is what you'd invent if you believed this diagram

If the only whole-body "up" dial is a hormone signal rather than a skin gene, then no topical cream, oral pill, or single melanocyte-restricted gene edit acting on the skin itself can raise pigment everywhere. The only way in is to inject the signal.

Melanotan II — sold unregulated online as a cosmetic tanning injection — is a synthetic, non-selective analog of α-MSH. It works by doing exactly what the diagram predicts: bypassing the skin and driving the melanocortin arm of cAMP → CREB → MITF directly. Its selective, FDA-approved cousin, afamelanotide, uses the same MC1R route medically, to treat light-sensitivity in erythropoietic protoporphyria.

Not medical advice. Melanotan II is unapproved for cosmetic use and carries documented adverse effects (nausea, and darkening of moles that can mask melanoma surveillance). It is used here purely to illustrate the mechanism the architecture predicts — afamelanotide is the regulated, MC1R-selective compound actually approved for clinical use.
Beat 4 — The evolutionary signature of the same asymmetry

The drug's target is disposable. The machine it triggers is not.

LOEUF = loss-of-function tolerance (gnomAD); higher = more mutation-tolerant. Across 803 network genes, synthesis-floor enzymes and the intracellular dial core show complete rank separation (Mann-Whitney U=64, p=1.55e-4, rank-biserial r=-1.00, FDR q=8e-4 on LOEUF; U=0, p=6.75e-4, r=1.00, FDR q=1.7e-3 on tissue breadth).

Synthesis-floor enzymes (TYR, TYRP1, DCT, OCA2, SLC45A2, MLANA, PMEL, SLC24A5) are the network's most loss-of-function-tolerant, tissue-restricted class: median LOEUF 1.13, expressed in only 5 of 54 tissue types. The intracellular MITF–cAMP–CREB dial core (MITF, CREB1, PRKACA, PRKAR1A, CREBBP, GNAS, ADCY3, ADCY6) is the opposite: median LOEUF 0.32, median pLI 1.00, expressed in all 54 tissues.

MC1R itself — the Melanotan target — has LOEUF 1.93, ranked 4th of 753 scored genes in the whole network (top 0.5% most LoF-tolerant), grouped with the disposable floor and with POMC (receptor/hormone layer median LOEUF 1.86) — not with the constrained core it activates (MITF LOEUF 0.48, pLI 0.992).

The gene you can safely mutate — and the one a cosmetic drug can safely target — is the doorbell. The transcription factor behind the door is welded shut. That's a structural reason Melanotan targets a receptor, never MITF directly.
Honest limits

This is a single-session synthesis over curated public resources (OMIM/GWAS disorder annotations, gnomAD constraint scores, a PMID-checked signalling edge list) — not new wet-lab data. The empty quadrant is a descriptive architecture, not a mechanistic proof, and the claim is scoped precisely to "no melanocyte-autonomous gene causes generalized hyperpigmentation" — 8 systemic-route genes exist and are shown, not hidden. The evolutionary comparison relies on one constraint metric (LOEUF/pLI) as a proxy for selective flexibility, and primate coat-color dichromatism (a separate, independently-evidenced track) is deliberately excluded from this argument.

The 3 questions

What did Claude Science help us do?

Reclassified ~200 disorder genes across three re-annotation passes, catching a ~3× gene-count inflation from vitiligo/VKH GWAS gene swarms by collapsing to real disease entities; ran the Mann-Whitney rank-separation tests on the 803-gene constraint table; PMID-verified every GPER1/estrogen signalling edge; and cross-checked the quadrant against three adversarial passes before it was trusted as the flagship.

How are we closer to a finding?

We went from "pigmentation genes are complicated" to a falsifiable structural claim with a clean empty cell and a statistically complete rank separation on two independent constraint metrics — one that also explains, mechanistically, why an existing unregulated pharmaceutical (Melanotan) targets a receptor and not a transcription factor.

Flagship

What is the flagship?

The empty quadrant (23 melanocyte-autonomous generalized-hypo genes, 0 hyper) plus the single convergent up-dial (cAMP → CREB → MITF, fed by two hormone axes) plus its evolutionary signature (disposable MC1R receptor vs. locked-down MITF core) — unified as: you can knock pigment down with a gene, but you can only dial it up with a hormone.
