BEAT 3 · WHY THIS SHAPE EXISTS

This asymmetry is the same one the disorder map already showed.

Sorting ~200 OMIM/GWAS pigmentation-disorder genes (→ 6 real disease entities after collapsing GWAS swarms) by direction × spread: 23 genes / 6 entities of melanocyte-autonomous + generalized disease are 100% hypopigmentation, 0% hyper. No melanocyte-autonomous gene turns pigment UP everywhere — only systemic hormone/metabolic routes do (8 genes, 2 entities: ACTH-excess, Wilson/ATP7B).

Population skin-colour variation in humans is uniform, not patchy — so natural selection only ever had the cell-autonomous synthesis floor to tune. The classic light-skin loci (SLC24A5, SLC45A2, OCA2, TYR) are hypomorphic alleles of the very genes that cause albinism when fully broken. There was never a "turn it up everywhere" lever to select on synthesis genes — exactly what the empty quadrant shows, and exactly why the LOEUF split lands where it does: the floor enzymes could afford to drift, the whole-body dial could not.

Honest scope: the mechanism-direction pattern (LoF of a positive regulator → less pigment) is close to definitional for albinism genes, not a novel biological law — we present it as architecture, not discovery. Primate coat-colour dichromatism is a separate, polygenic story (MC1R is not the hit there) and is not merged into this constraint claim.
BEAT 4 · THE PAYOFF, SOLD ON THE INTERNET

Melanotan is this asymmetry, injected.

Melanotan II is a synthetic, non-selective analog of α-MSH, sold unregulated online for cosmetic tanning — it works by injecting the ligand for the one receptor evolution kept easy to perturb (LOEUF 1.93) to drive the one downstream machine evolution never let drift (LOEUF 0.32–0.48). No cream or topical acts on skin cells directly to raise pigment uniformly, because the only whole-body "up" dial is hormonal, not dermal — the disposable switch, not the locked engine, is the accessible entry point. Its FDA-approved, MC1R-selective cousin afamelanotide treats erythropoietic protoporphyria through the identical node.

Safety disclaimer: Melanotan II is unapproved, non-selective, and has documented adverse effects including nausea and darkening of moles that can mask melanoma monitoring. Presented here strictly for mechanistic illustration — not medical advice, not an endorsement.
BEAT 5 · A SECOND HAND ON THE SAME DIAL

Even the estrogen route doesn't get its own engine.

A second, independent hormone axis converges on the identical valve. Estrogen signals to melanocytes via the membrane receptor GPER1 (a verified melanocyte cis-eGene, q≈0.005) — not ERα (melanocytes are ERα-negative by IHC) — raising cAMP and funnelling into the same cAMP→CREB→MITF node the melanocortin arm uses. That's the biology behind melasma / pregnancy hyperpigmentation. Two hormone arms, one valve: it reinforces the picture that the entire network was built around a single constrained output node, approached from multiple disposable entry points.

What did Claude Science help us do?

Merged a 803-gene evolutionary feature table (LOEUF/pLI/tissue breadth/missense-z) with the manually re-annotated 200-gene disorder set, ran the constrained group-vs-floor statistics, and cross-checked the MC1R rank against the full scored distribution — all recomputed this session, not asserted from memory.

How are we closer to a finding?

We moved from "MC1R is hypervariable" (known) to a quantified, ranked claim: MC1R's evolutionary tolerance is in the top 0.5% of the entire network, and separates completely (rank-biserial r=−1.00) from the intracellular machinery it triggers — a number that didn't exist before this session's analysis.

What is the flagship?

The pathway is evolutionarily lopsided by design: a cheap, tunable receptor gate (MC1R, POMC) feeding an intolerant, ubiquitously-expressed intracellular engine (MITF/CREB1/PRKACA/PRKAR1A/CREBBP/GNAS) — the same asymmetry the empty disorder-quadrant and the Melanotan mechanism both independently point to.
