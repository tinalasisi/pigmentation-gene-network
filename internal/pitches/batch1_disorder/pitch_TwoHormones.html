The Flagship Result

Two hormones. One valve.

Melanocortin (via MC1R) and estrogen (via GPER1) are two independent hormone systems that converge on the identical cAMP→CREB→MITF node — the only whole-body switch that turns human pigment up. Melasma in pregnancy and a black-market tanning peptide are the same biological event, read through two different hands on one dial.

Fig. 1 — Convergence architecture. Two ligand→receptor arms (melanocortin→MC1R, estrogen→GPER1) both raise intracellular cAMP and drive the same CREB→MITF transcriptional program. Downstream, one biology explains two real-world observations: hormonal hyperpigmentation (melasma/pregnancy) and a pharmacological tanning agent (Melanotan).
BEAT 1 / 4
Why a hormone at all?

There is no cell-autonomous gene that switches pigment up, body-wide.

Sorting ~200 OMIM/GWAS pigmentation-disorder genes by direction × spread × cell-of-origin, one cell is empirically empty:

  • Melanocyte-autonomous + generalized: 23 genes / 6 entities → 100% hypopigmentation, 0% hyperpigmentation. (Albinism/OCA, Hermansky–Pudlak, Chediak–Higashi, Griscelli, Tietz/MITF.)
  • Restrict to patchy spread and the asymmetry flips (28 hyper vs 7 hypo) — it is specific to "generalized."
  • The only generalized-hyper rows are not melanocyte genes: 6 endocrine/adrenal-ACTH-excess entities and 2 metabolic (Wilson disease / ATP7B) — both act through a systemic signal, not a skin gene.
You can switch human pigment off everywhere with a single melanocyte gene. No melanocyte gene switches it on everywhere — only a circulating hormone can. That leaves exactly the melanocortin route (α-MSH→MC1R) as the known whole-body "up" dial — before we even add the second hand.
BEAT 2 / 4
The new piece this session adds

Estrogen reaches the same valve — through a different door.

  • The estrogen arm signals through the membrane receptor GPER1, a verified melanocyte cis-eGene (q ≈ 0.005) — not the classical nuclear receptor ESR1/ERα, which is excluded (melanocytes are ERα-negative by IHC).
  • GPER1 activation raises intracellular cAMP, feeding into the identical cAMP→CREB→MITF node that the melanocortin (MC1R) arm already uses to drive pigment synthesis.
  • Every estrogen→melanocyte edge used here was checked against a live PubMed lookup (PMIDs verified) before being trusted — not taken on a database label alone.
This gives melasma / pregnancy hyperpigmentation a mechanism, not just a clinical association: a second, independent hormone input converging on the one node the disorder map already flagged as the sole "up" dial.
BEAT 3 / 4
The architecture, sold on the internet

Melanotan is the melanocortin hand of the valve, injected.

  • Melanotan II is a synthetic, non-selective analog of α-MSH, sold unregulated online for cosmetic tanning — it works by injecting the one whole-body pigment-up signal the architecture predicts must exist.
  • Its FDA-approved, MC1R-selective cousin, afamelanotide, treats erythropoietic protoporphyria — the legitimate, receptor-selective version of the same mechanism.
  • No topical, oral, or gene-editing approach turns skin pigment up uniformly by acting on the skin itself — because that lever, per Beat 1, does not exist at the cell-autonomous level.
Safety, stated plainly: Melanotan II as sold online is unapproved, non-selective, and has documented adverse effects (nausea, and darkening of moles that can mask melanoma surveillance). It is discussed here only to illustrate the mechanism identified above — this is not medical advice, and afamelanotide is the separate, regulated compound.
BEAT 4 / 4
The twist underneath both hands

The receptors on the valve are disposable. The valve itself is not.

  • Across the 803-gene network, synthesis-floor enzymes and the intracellular MITF–cAMP–CREB dial core separate completely on evolutionary constraint (LOEUF: Mann-Whitney U=64, p=1.55e-4, rank-biserial r=−1.00, FDR q=8e-4; tissue breadth: U=0, p=6.75e-4, r=1.00, FDR q=1.7e-3).
  • MC1R — the Melanotan target — has LOEUF 1.93, ranked 4th of 753 scored network genes (top 0.5% most LoF-tolerant), grouped with the disposable receptor/hormone layer (POMC-layer median LOEUF 1.86).
  • MITF, the node both hormone arms converge on, sits in the constrained tail: LOEUF 0.48, pLI 0.992, expressed in 54/54 tissues scored — alongside CREB1, PRKACA, GNAS, ADCY3/6.
Two independent hormones can afford to evolve or vary at their receptor doors (MC1R coding hypervariability, red-hair alleles, individual GPER1 sensitivity) precisely because the shared downstream machine they both must hit is fixed and non-negotiable.
Honest limits
GPER1→cAMP is an eQTL-supported, PMID-checked convergence argument, not a new wet-lab causal proof. The disorder-quadrant asymmetry (Beat 1) generalizes across melanocyte-autonomous genes broken by loss-of-function; it does not claim no gene at all causes generalized hyperpigmentation (8 systemic-route genes do, via a hormone signal). Primate coat-colour evolution is a separate evidence track and is not merged into this claim.

The flagship

Two hormones, one valve: melanocortin and estrogen are separate evolutionary lineages funneled into one constrained transcriptional switch. Melasma, pregnancy hyperpigmentation, and a black-market tanning drug are three faces of the same biology.
