You can switch human pigment off everywhere with a melanocyte gene.
No melanocyte gene switches it up everywhere.

The only whole-body "up" dial left standing is a diffusible hormone signal: α-MSH → MC1R → cAMP → CREB → MITF. A second, independent hormone axis converges on that same final valve: estrogen reaches melanocytes through the membrane receptor GPER1 (verified melanocyte cis-eGene, q≈0.005) — not ERα, which melanocytes don't express — raising cAMP into the identical node. Two hands, one switch. That's the biology behind melasma and pregnancy hyperpigmentation, and it's the target the next act hijacks directly.

Act II — Pharmacy

The prediction, sold on the internet.

If the clinic-wide architecture is real, there should be no cream, pill, or topical gene therapy that tans skin uniformly by acting on skin — because that lever does not exist in the disorder map. The only way to darken everywhere is to inject the hormone signal itself. That is exactly the product being sold, unregulated, online.

The drug is the architecture's prediction, sold on the internet — minus the safety rails.
Pharmacology-as-illustration, not medical advice. Melanotan II is an unapproved, non-selective compound sold outside regulated channels, with documented adverse effects including nausea and darkening of moles that can mask melanoma surveillance. Afamelanotide is a separate, FDA-approved, MC1R-selective drug for a specific rare disorder (EPP). It is discussed here only to illustrate the mechanism predicted by Act I, not as a recommendation of either compound.
Act III — Deep Time

Evolution had the same problem — and only the same lever.

Population-level skin-colour variation is itself whole-body, so natural selection faced exactly the constraint the clinic map exposes: there was no gene that turns pigment up uniformly. Evolution could only tune the melanocyte-autonomous synthesis floor. The classic loci under selection for lighter skin — SLC24A5, SLC45A2, OCA2, TYR — are the same genes that cause albinism when fully broken. Derived light-skin alleles are hypomorphs: partial-loss versions of the albinism genes, not a new "darken" switch running in reverse. (MFSD12 sits in the same floor pathway, but its own loss-of-function reduces pheomelanin and tracks with darker, not lighter, skin — the floor has locus-specific direction, not a uniform dimmer knob.)

That is the same architecture from Act I, seen from evolutionary time: MC1R and the synthesis floor are the parts biology (and pharmacology) is free to vary — disposable at the edges. MITF and the intracellular dial it anchors are the part nothing gets to touch. Disease can only turn it fully off (Act I). A drug can only reach it from outside, through the receptor (Act II). Selection could only ever dim the floor beneath it (Act III). Same node, three vantage points.

Honest limits. The clinical direction-law ("loss of a positive melanin regulator → less pigment") is close to the definition of albinism — it demonstrates the classification method, not a new biological law. One gene (ATP7B/Wilson disease) is the sole miss in an n=4 failure-boundary check, which is too small to claim "confidence flags misses" as a general rule. Primate coat-colour dichromatism (15× independent origins, always polygenic) is a separate evidence track from a different dataset and is not merged into the verified disorder-architecture claim above.
For the judges

Three questions, answered.

What did Claude Science help us do

Re-annotated ~200 OMIM/GWAS disorder-gene rows into real clinical entities (correcting a ~3× inflation from vitiligo/VKH GWAS gene swarms), ran three adversarial verification passes on the resulting classification, computed LOEUF / pLI / tissue-breadth rank statistics across an 803-gene network, and cross-checked every sex-hormone convergence edge against a live PubMed lookup.

How are we closer to a finding

Scattered case reports, a GWAS catalogue, a constraint table, and a pharmacology footnote don't normally talk to each other. Structuring them by direction × spread × cell-of-origin turned them into one falsifiable structural claim — a genuinely empty cell — and then a statistically clean, complete rank separation (both p<10⁻³, FDR-corrected) that explains, mechanistically, why an unregulated internet drug works exactly the way the clinic map predicts.

What is the flagship

The empty melanocyte-autonomous / generalized / hyperpigmentation cell (23 genes, 6 entities, 0 hyper) and the single cAMP→CREB→MITF dial it exposes — now shown to be pharmacologically fireable from outside the skin (Melanotan) and evolutionarily untouchable at its core, with only the disposable receptor (MC1R) and disposable floor enzymes free to vary.
