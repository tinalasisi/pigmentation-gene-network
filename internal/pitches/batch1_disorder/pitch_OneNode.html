One node to rule them all.

Every biological "turn skin pigment up" signal — a hormone, a second hormone, or a drug sold on the internet — is forced through the exact same three-letter relay: cAMP → CREB → MITF. There is no other way in.

We mapped ~200 pigmentation-disorder genes, a sex-hormone signalling layer, and the evolutionary constraint on 803 network genes onto one picture. All three point at the same bottleneck.

The hero result

Three inputs. One valve. Convergence isn't a metaphor here — it's the finding.

Two independent hormone systems (melanocortin, estrogen) and one recreational drug all dead-end at the same three-gene relay. That's not three separate mechanisms — it's one mechanism with three doorbells.

Why we know it's the only door

We didn't assume convergence. We ruled out every alternative first.

Disorder architecture · ~200 OMIM/GWAS genes
23 / 0
23 melanocyte-autonomous, generalized-pigmentation genes (6 real disease entities) — 100% of them cause hypopigmentation. Zero cause generalized hyperpigmentation.

You can switch human pigment off everywhere with one broken melanocyte gene. No melanocyte gene switches it on everywhere — that lever isn't in the genome.

The evolutionary twist

The valve is locked down. The button that presses it is disposable.

Across 803 network genes ranked by evolutionary constraint (LOEUF: lower = less tolerant of loss), the intracellular cAMP–CREB–MITF core and the receptor layer that feeds it occupy opposite ends of the distribution — with a complete rank separation between them.

Complete rank separation between the two gene classes: Mann-Whitney U=64, p=1.55×10-4, rank-biserial r=−1.00, FDR q=8×10-4 on LOEUF; U=0, p=6.75×10-4, r=1.00, FDR q=1.7×10-3 on tissue breadth. MC1R sits with the disposable receptor/floor layer it activates, not with the machine it triggers.

The pharmacological proof

Melanotan is the architecture's prediction, already sold on the internet.

If the only whole-body "pigment up" dial is a hormone signal into cAMP→CREB→MITF — not a skin gene — then the only way to tan uniformly without sun is to inject that signal, not apply something to skin. That's exactly what Melanotan II does: a synthetic, non-selective analog of α-MSH that drives the melanocortin arm of the same node.

Melanotan II — unapproved, sold online, non-selective
Afamelanotide ("Melanotan I") — FDA-approved, MC1R-selective, treats erythropoietic protoporphyria
Safety note: Melanotan II is not FDA-approved and carries documented adverse effects (nausea, darkening of moles that can mask melanoma monitoring). Presented here for mechanism illustration only — not medical advice. Afamelanotide is the separate, approved, receptor-selective compound.

Honest limits

The disorder count is by real disease entities, not raw gene rows — collapsing GWAS swarms (e.g. vitiligo/VKH) from ~3× inflated gene counts down to 6 true entities was a human correction layered onto the first pass. The mechanism-to-direction rule for melanocyte genes is a methodological check, not a new biological law — "losing a positive melanin regulator causes hypopigmentation" is close to the definition of albinism. Two systemic, non-melanocyte routes (endocrine ACTH excess, Wilson disease copper toxicity) do reach generalized hyperpigmentation — the claim is "no melanocyte-autonomous gene," never "no gene at all." Primate coat-colour dichromatism (polygenic, independent losses of MC1R's role) is a separate evidence track and is not merged into this flagship.

Rubric, answered

What did Claude Science help us do?

Re-annotated and adversarially re-checked ~200 disorder genes into a direction × spread × cell-of-origin grid, PMID-verified a second hormone-signalling layer (NB13), and ranked 803 network genes by evolutionary constraint — then read the same convergence point off all three independent analyses.

How are we closer to a finding?

We went from "MITF is a pigmentation gene" to a falsifiable structural claim: every characterized whole-body up-regulating input funnels through cAMP→CREB→MITF, and that node is the most evolutionarily constrained part of the entire network — while its own upstream receptor (MC1R) is among the most tolerant genes scored. That is a testable architecture, not a gene list.

What is the flagship?

The convergence diagram itself: one locked valve, three doorbells — melanocortin, estrogen, and a drug bought online — all pressing the same button, on a node evolution will not let you touch.
