THE NETWORK MADE IT VISIBLE — Pigmentation Gene Network Life-Sciences Hackathon · Researcher Track
Flagship Finding

No single gene switches human pigment UP everywhere.
The network is the only reason we could see that.

A curated, human-checked, 806-node pigmentation network exposed one empty biological quadrant — invisible in any gene list, invisible in any single paper — and traced it to one converging molecular dial: cAMP → CREB → MITF.

The method is the finding. Individual OMIM entries don't show this. Individual GWAS hits don't show this. It only became visible once ~200 disorder genes were re-annotated on a shared scheme — direction × spread × cell-of-origin — cross-checked against a 803-gene evidence network built from mass-spec, CRISPR screens, transcription-factor regulons, and PMID-verified literature edges, and adversarially re-tested three times.

Beat 1Why raw gene counts would have LIED to us

A first pass over public disorder annotations counts genes, not diseases — and vitiligo/VKH-type GWAS swarms inflate gene tallies roughly 3× versus real clinical entities. Collapsing 23 genes correctly down to 6 real disease entities (Hermansky-Pudlak, Chediak-Higashi, Griscelli, Albinism/OCA, MC1R-RHC, Tietz/MITF) was a human editorial call layered onto the first machine draft — not a default the tool gave us for free. Skip that correction and the quadrant looks like statistical noise from an over-counted syndrome family, not a real biological wall.

Beat 2 — HeroTwo independent hormone layers, PMID-checked edge by edge, land on ONE node

NB13 built a separate, PMID-checked evidence layer for sex-hormone signaling into melanocytes. Every one of its edges was checked against a live literature lookup before being trusted — including the negative result that ESR1 is excluded (melanocytes are ERα-negative by IHC). The estrogen arm instead reaches the cell through the membrane receptor GPER1 (verified melanocyte cis-eGene, q≈0.005). Laid over the melanocortin arm from the disorder network, the two hormone axes are structurally independent all the way down to their receptors — and converge on the exact same terminal node.

Fig. 2 — Convergence node. Melanocortin (α-MSH→MC1R) and estrogen (→GPER1) are independent upstream axes; both funnel into cAMP→CREB→MITF, the single terminal valve for whole-body pigment output.

This is the piece that a single-pathway diagram would never show: two receptor systems that never touch each other converge only at the last step. You cannot see "convergence" from one pathway map — it is a property of the union of two independently-verified network layers, checked against each other.

More than one hand on the switch. Only one switch.

Beat 3The network's prediction is already sold on the internet

If the network's structure is right — one whole-body "up" dial, reached only through a hormone receptor, never through a skin-resident synthesis gene — then the only way to tan uniformly without UV is to inject the hormone signal, not to modify skin cells directly. That is exactly what Melanotan II does: a synthetic, non-selective analog of α-MSH, unregulated, sold online for cosmetic tanning, activating the same MC1R→cAMP→CREB→MITF route the network flags as the sole generalized-hyperpigmentation dial.

Its FDA-approved, MC1R-selective relative, afamelanotide, treats erythropoietic protoporphyria — a legitimate, regulated use of the same mechanism.

Pharmacology-as-illustration, not medical advice. Melanotan II sold online is unapproved and non-selective, with documented adverse effects (nausea, and darkening of moles that can mask melanoma monitoring). Afamelanotide is a separate, FDA-approved, MC1R-selective compound. We cite Melanotan only to show that the network's structural prediction — "the dial is a hormone, not a skin gene" — already exists as a real-world pharmacological product.

Beat 4The network also shows WHY the dial stayed switchable

Ranking all 753 scored genes in the network by evolutionary constraint (LOEUF — lower = more constrained) produces a complete rank separation between two mechanistic classes inside the same pathway:

Fig. 3 — Constraint landscape. Synthesis-floor enzymes and the MC1R/POMC receptor layer sit in the LoF-tolerant zone; the intracellular MITF–cAMP–CREB core sits in the constrained, ubiquitously-expressed zone. Complete rank separation (Mann-Whitney U=64, p=1.55e-4, rank-biserial r=-1.00).

Synthesis-floor enzymes (n=8)

TYR, TYRP1, DCT, OCA2, SLC45A2, MLANA, PMEL, SLC24A5 — median LOEUF 1.13, tissue breadth 5/54. The most disposable, most tissue-restricted class.

MC1R — the Melanotan target

LOEUF 1.93, rank 4 of 753 scored network genes (top 0.5% most LoF-tolerant) — grouped with the disposable floor and POMC, not with the machine it activates.

Intracellular dial core (n=8)

MITF, CREB1, PRKACA, PRKAR1A, CREBBP, GNAS, ADCY3, ADCY6 — median LOEUF 0.32, pLI 1.00, tissue breadth 54/54. The most constrained, most ubiquitous class in the whole network.

The receptor you can drug is disposable. The machine it triggers is locked down. That gap is only visible by ranking the whole network at once — not by looking at MC1R alone.

LimitsWhat the network does NOT let us claim

Scope, stated plainly: "No melanocyte-autonomous gene causes generalized hyperpigmentation" — not "no gene at all" (8 systemic/endocrine-route rows exist and are shown above). The mechanism→direction pattern in the broader network (loss of a positive melanin regulator → hypopigmentation) is a methodological demonstration, not a biological discovery — it is close to the definition of albinism. One boundary case (ATP7B/Wilson disease) breaks a clean "confidence flags misses" reading, so we don't claim that. Primate dichromatism and cross-species melanism are a separate evidence track, built from a different dataset, and are not merged into this disorder-architecture claim.

Rubric

What did Claude Science help us do?

Built and cross-checked an 806-node, PMID-annotated pigmentation network from mass-spec, CRISPR, TF-regulon, and literature layers; ran the entity-vs-gene correction; executed three adversarial verification passes against the disorder classification; and ranked evolutionary constraint across all 753 scored network genes in one coherent pass — work no single paper or spreadsheet does at once.

How are we closer to a finding?

The empty quadrant (23 genes / 6 entities, 0 exceptions) and the two-hormone convergence on cAMP→CREB→MITF are not visible in any one data source — they are properties of the merged, curated network, verified against each other. That is a structural claim about pigmentation biology, not a restated fact.

What is the flagship?

The convergence figure: two independently-verified hormone axes, checked edge-by-edge against literature, meeting at one terminal dial — with the disorder architecture, Melanotan, and the constraint landscape as three lines of corroborating evidence around it.
