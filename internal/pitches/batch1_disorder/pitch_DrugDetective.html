Case File · Pigmentation Genetics

THE DRUG THAT
SHOULDN'T WORK

Melanotan II is an unregulated tanning injectable sold on the internet — and it works. No topical cream, no pill, no single skin gene can raise human pigment uniformly. So how does a back-alley peptide manage what a century of dermatology hasn't? We reverse-engineered the answer from a systematic scan of 200 pigmentation-disorder genes: there is exactly one whole-body "up" dial in the human genome, and the drug found it before we did.

EXHIBIT A

The Clue: A Peptide That Ignores the Rulebook

Melanotan II is a synthetic, non-selective analog of the pituitary hormone α-MSH, self-injected for cosmetic tanning outside any regulatory approval. Its MC1R-selective, FDA-approved cousin — afamelanotide — treats a rare light-sensitivity disorder, erythropoietic protoporphyria.

Neither drug touches the skin. Both act on a single receptor, MC1R, and both darken the entire body uniformly — something no gene therapy, cream, or supplement aimed at pigment-producing machinery has ever achieved. That's the anomaly a detective would flag: the intervention that shouldn't generalize, generalizes.

Used here for mechanism illustration only — not medical advice. Melanotan II carries documented adverse effects (nausea, mole darkening that can mask melanoma monitoring) precisely because it is non-selective and unsupervised.

EXHIBIT B

The Search: Interrogating 200 Disorder Genes

If Melanotan's trick is real, the genome itself should show the same asymmetry. We sorted ~200 OMIM/GWAS pigmentation-disorder genes (collapsed to real clinical entities, not inflated GWAS-hit counts) by direction (less vs. more pigment) × spread (whole-body vs. patchy) × cell of origin.

Genes acting inside the melanocyte itself — synthesis and melanosome machinery — can shut pigment off everywhere: 23 genes / 6 entities (albinism, Hermansky-Pudlak, Chediak-Higashi, Griscelli, Tietz) are melanocyte-autonomous, generalized, and 100% hypopigmentation. The same class of gene, when it disrupts pigment only patchily, flips freely (28 hyper / 7 hypo) — the asymmetry is specific to "generalized."

Zero melanocyte-autonomous genes drive generalized hyperpigmentation. The only whole-body "up" rows in the entire dataset are systemic-hormonal (ACTH/adrenal excess) or systemic-metabolic (Wilson disease copper toxicity) — never a skin-cell gene turned up. (Not "no gene at all" — those 8 systemic-route rows exist; no melanocyte gene does it.)

EXHIBIT C

The Second Witness: An Independent Hormone Corroborates

A single empty quadrant could be coincidence. So we looked for a second, unrelated hormonal route into pigment: estrogen. Melanocytes are ERα-negative by IHC — the classical estrogen receptor is excluded. Instead, estrogen signals through the membrane receptor GPER1, a verified melanocyte cis-eGene (q ≈ 0.005), every edge checked against a live PubMed lookup.

GPER1 raises cAMP — funnelling into the identical cAMP→CREB→MITF node that MC1R uses. This is the real biology behind melasma and pregnancy hyperpigmentation. Two independent hormone axes, discovered on two separate tracks of this project, converge on one final valve.

Two hands can reach the switch. There is still only one switch.

EXHIBIT D

The Twist: Why the Drug Target Is the Cheap Lock

A detective's last question: why is this the exploitable seam? We ranked all 753 scored genes in the pigmentation network by evolutionary constraint (LOEUF — lower = less tolerant of loss-of-function).

The intracellular dial core — MITF, CREB1, PRKACA, PRKAR1A, CREBBP, GNAS, ADCY3, ADCY6 — is the single most constrained, most ubiquitously-expressed class in the network: median LOEUF 0.32, tissue breadth 54/54. The synthesis-floor enzymes (TYR, OCA2, SLC45A2…) sit at the opposite pole: median LOEUF 1.13, restricted to ~5/54 tissues. Complete rank separation (Mann-Whitney U=64, p=1.55e-4, FDR q=8e-4).

MC1R — the exact receptor Melanotan hijacks — has LOEUF 1.93, rank 4 of 753 genes: the top 0.5% most loss-of-function-tolerant gene in the entire network. It sits with the disposable receptor/hormone layer, not with the locked-down machine it triggers. Evolution left the doorknob loose so it could keep the furniture bolted down. A drug that finds the one hypervariable receptor sitting on top of the one rigid pathway isn't luck — it's exploiting the network's own constraint architecture.

MC1R activates the dial core it evolutionarily has nothing in common with — the disposable switch for the indispensable machine.

VERDICT

Case Closed: The Drug Is the Architecture's Prediction

No skin-gene therapy, no melanocyte-targeted cream can raise pigment everywhere, because the genome contains exactly one whole-body "up" dial — cAMP→CREB→MITF — and it is reached only through hormone receptors, never through synthesis machinery itself. Melanotan II is not a clever hack around biology; it is biology's only available lever, discovered on the street before it was diagrammed in a lab. The empty quadrant (23 genes / 6 entities of melanocyte-autonomous hypopigmentation, zero of melanocyte-autonomous hyperpigmentation), the convergent estrogen witness, and the evolutionary-constraint asymmetry (dial core LOEUF 0.32 vs. its disposable trigger MC1R at LOEUF 1.93, top 0.5%) are three independent lines of evidence for the same single mechanism.

Safety note: Melanotan II as discussed here is unapproved and non-selective, with documented adverse effects including nausea and mole darkening that can mask melanoma monitoring. Its FDA-approved, MC1R-selective analog (afamelanotide) exists precisely because selectivity matters. Nothing here is medical advice; the drug is used strictly to illustrate a mechanism already visible in the genetics.

Rubric

What did Claude Science help us do

Re-annotate and adversarially re-check ~200 OMIM/GWAS disorder-gene entries and 803 network genes' evolutionary-constraint scores in one session, then connect that structural pattern to a real drug's documented mechanism — work that would otherwise mean weeks of literature triage across genetics, endocrinology, and pharmacology.

How are we closer to a finding

We didn't just observe an odd drug that works — we showed why it has to be the one that works: it is the only pharmacological route onto the genome's single unconstrained entry point into an otherwise-locked pigment-control circuit, corroborated by an independent hormone axis (GPER1/estrogen).

What is the flagship

The empty quadrant: 23 vs. 0 melanocyte-autonomous disorder entities, generalized hypo- vs. hyperpigmentation — now explained mechanistically by receptor-vs-relay constraint asymmetry, and demonstrated pharmacologically by a drug already in circulation.

Honest limits: the disorder-quadrant asymmetry is a pattern in curated OMIM/GWAS entities, not a controlled experiment; "no melanocyte-autonomous gene" is not "no gene at all" — 8 systemic-route (ACTH/Wilson-disease) rows exist outside the melanocyte. Evolutionary-constraint values (LOEUF, rank 4/753) are gnomAD population-genetic summaries, not direct measures of selection on pigmentation itself.
