# Two-tier plan: network-topology-aware selection analysis on the primate pigmentation panel

Scope: use the network TOPOLOGY of the signed, directed melanogenesis GRN (`data/processed/gene_network_nodes.csv` / `gene_network_edges.csv`, 168 nodes / 309 edges) to structure the interpretation of the cross-species selection statistics already computed for the ~80-gene panel (`comparative-genomics/results/full_panel_117/`), rather than treating each gene independently or aggregating by pigmentation/hormone label. This is distinct from `comparative-genomics/analysis/module_selection/` (per-origin pigmentation-vs-hormone gene-SET balance) and from `internal/network-evo-explore/` (network centrality vs human population constraint) — neither uses the cross-species selection signal together with the GRN edge structure.

## Ground truth established by direct inspection

- **Panel coverage in the network**: of the 77 genes with a RELAX result, only **16** appear as nodes in the 168-node signed directed GRN (`DCT, EDNRB, KIT, KITLG, LEF1, MC1R, MITF, MLANA, OCA2, PAX3, PMEL, POMC, SOX10, TFAP2A, TYR, TYRP1`). The other 61 panel genes (all the hormone-axis genes — steroidogenic enzymes, receptors, nuclear coactivators — plus most late-pathway pigmentation genes) are simply absent from this GRN; they were never curated into it.
- **Within-panel edges**: of the 309 edges, **15 directed, non-self edges** connect two panel genes that are both network nodes (plus 2 self-loop rows for MITF autoregulation, excluded). This collapses to **15 unique undirected gene pairs** among **13** of the 16 panel genes present (LEF1, POMC, TFAP2A have no edge to another panel gene and drop out of any edge-level test). All 15 edges are signed `+` and are `mechanistic_projection` edges (curated/inherited, not raw STRING). The subgraph is strongly hub-centered: MITF degree 10, SOX10 degree 5, everything else degree 1–2 (KIT, KITLG, MC1R, EDNRB, MLANA, OCA2, PMEL, PAX3, TYR, TYRP1, DCT).
- **Sensitivity check** against the denser 803-node multilayer substrate (`nb7_substrate_edges.csv`, which also folds in STRING/OmniPath/GRN-regulon evidence): the same 13 genes, but **18** non-self within-panel edges (PAX3 additionally links to DCT/TYR/TYRP1 through the regulon layer, not present in the curated core network). The connected-gene set does not change; only the edge count grows modestly.
- **n is genuinely small.** Any edge-level topology test is being run on a 13-node, 15–18-edge induced subgraph. This is stated up front because it bounds what any p-value below can mean — see Confounds.
- **aBSREL per-branch coverage** is incomplete: of the 13 within-panel-edge genes, only **7** have a non-empty `aBSREL.json` (EDNRB, KIT, MITF, MLANA, OCA2, SOX10, TYRP1); the other 6 (DCT, KITLG, MC1R, PAX3, PMEL, TYR) are 0-byte files — failed or not-yet-run aBSREL jobs. Branch attribute keys are per-gene-tree tip/internal-node labels (species names for tips, `NodeN` for internal nodes); tip names are directly comparable across genes (same species), internal node numbering is not.
- **Per-origin RELAX** (`results/perorigin_v1/per_origin_K.csv`) has been completed for only **3 of the 14** independent dichromatism origins (origin_7, origin_8, origin_14); the other 11 have not been run.

## TIER 1 — runnable now, locally, from frozen outputs

### Recommended test: GRN-neighbor selection-similarity permutation

**Question.** Do genes that are direct regulator→effector partners in the signed melanogenesis GRN show more correlated cross-species selection intensity (RELAX K) than genes that are not directly connected?

**Why this candidate over the alternatives offered:**
- (b) "regulator→effector edges show coordinated intensification" is a directional special case of (a) and is reported as a secondary, purely descriptive breakdown below — it does not need its own null model.
- (c) network propagation of the aBSREL per-branch signal needs a densely-scored, densely-connected substrate. We have neither: only 13 panel genes are network-connected at all, and only 7 of those have any aBSREL signal. Diffusing a per-branch score across a 13-node graph with 6 missing nodes is not a defensible propagation analysis — it is listed in `feasibility_matrix.csv` as **tier: none** for that reason.
- (a) is therefore the only candidate with enough real structure (15 edges, closed graph, complete K for all 13 nodes) to run today without generating new data.

**Exact statistic.** Build the induced subgraph on the 13 within-panel-connected genes (15 undirected edges from the core GRN; primary analysis). For each unordered gene pair (i,j), compute a selection-distance:
```
d(i,j) = | log(K_i) - log(K_j) |
```
(log because K is a ratio centered at K=1 — the semantic null of "no relaxation/intensification shift"; this keeps K=0.5 and K=2 symmetric distances from K=1). Define the test statistic as the difference in mean distance between non-edge pairs and edge pairs:
```
T_obs = mean_d(non-adjacent pairs) - mean_d(adjacent pairs)
```
`T_obs > 0` means GRN neighbors have MORE similar (correlated) selection signal than non-neighbors — the topology-coordination hypothesis. Report `T_obs`, the two group means, and n per group (15 adjacent pairs vs 78-15=63 non-adjacent pairs among C(13,2)=78 total pairs).

**Null model.** Degree-preserving edge permutation (configuration-model double-edge-swap rewiring) of the 13-node/15-edge subgraph: repeatedly swap edge endpoints while holding each node's degree fixed, recompute `T` on the rewired graph with the SAME (unpermuted) K values attached to nodes, 10,000 rewirings, empirical two-sided p = fraction of |T_rewired| >= |T_obs|. This is the correct null for "is the SPECIFIC WIRING PATTERN informative, given this hub-dominated degree sequence" — as opposed to a naive label permutation, which would ignore that MITF's degree of 10 makes its neighbors inherently more likely to look "close" by chance regardless of biology. Run the same test on the 18-edge substrate-network subgraph as a robustness check; a result that only holds in one network, not both, should be reported as topology-dependent, not treated as confirmed.

**Secondary, descriptive breakdown (no formal null needed, report as exploratory).** Split the 13 genes by GRN role: regulators that are edge SOURCES (MITF, SOX10, PAX3) vs their direct targets (10 distinct effector genes). Report each group's K distribution. This is motivated by the pleiotropy-dilution logic already established in this project for hub/housekeeping genes: MITF itself is pooled-K = 0.61 (relaxed, not a certified hit) while several of its direct targets are intensified (TYR K=2.18, certified-tier signal in TYR; KITLG K=1.30, certified hit; PAX3 K=1.41) and others are relaxed (MC1R K=0.45, MLANA K=0.39, OCA2 K=0.57). This split is worth reporting explicitly whether or not it reaches significance, because it is the observation a reviewer will ask about first, and because it is consistent with (not proof of) the network's own hub-dilution hypothesis.

**Expected n.** 13 nodes, 15 edges (78 total pairs, 15 adjacent / 63 non-adjacent), core network; 18 edges on the substrate sensitivity check. All 13 nodes have complete pooled RELAX K (no missing data for this specific test — this is exactly why it is the Tier-1 candidate and the aBSREL/per-branch version is not).

**What a positive result means:** selection intensity/relaxation on dichromatism-associated lineages is not distributed independently across the melanogenesis network — it clusters along direct regulatory connections, consistent with coordinated evolutionary pressure propagating through the pathway (e.g. a change in a regulator's target-binding co-evolving with the target).

**What a negative (null) result means, and why that is still worth reporting:** with only 13 nodes and 15 edges, a null result cannot be read as strong evidence of NO topological coordination — it is a genuinely underpowered test for anything short of a large effect. The honest framing is: this is a well-defined, correctly-specified pilot test, not a well-powered confirmatory one; a null result here motivates Tier 2 (below), which trades a richer per-pair statistic — full-tree rate correlation rather than a single K ratio — for the same small edge count, and is the more defensible next move rather than concluding "no coordination" from n=15 edges alone.

## TIER 2 — a short cluster job

### Recommended test: pairwise coevolutionary-rate (mirrortree/ERC) test on GRN edges

**Rationale for choosing this over the PolySel-topology-module alternative.** `comparative-genomics/scripts/05_polysel_geneset.py` already exists and does exactly what its docstring says: a SUMSTAT/PolySel-style gene-SET selection test with a label-permutation null. Re-running it with GRN-topology-derived modules (e.g. TF-core: MITF/SOX10/PAX3 vs receptor: KIT/KITLG/EDNRB/MC1R vs structural/enzymatic: TYR/TYRP1/DCT/PMEL/MLANA in place of the hormone/pigmentation panel labels) is possible with zero new code, but the resampling background would still be only the same 13 network-connected, scored genes split three ways (3-5 genes/module) — a permutation null with that few genes per set is not going to produce a trustworthy p-value, and the underlying statistical shape (sum a per-gene score, permute labels) duplicates the fragility already documented for the existing hormone-vs-pigmentation SUMSTAT test (permutation p=0.87, not significant, per `coevolution_test.qmd`). It is listed in `feasibility_matrix.csv` as **tier: none** for this reason — it would run in minutes but wouldn't tell us anything the existing module_selection work hasn't already shown about small-n SUMSTAT fragility.

The coevolution/ERC route uses the SAME small edge count (15-18 true GRN edges) but replaces the single-scalar-per-gene comparison (RELAX K) with a full **per-branch rate vector** comparison for every gene pair — i.e., it asks whether two genes' molecular clocks speed up and slow down together across the SAME branches of the SAME tree, which is a substantially richer per-pair signal than comparing two summary K's. That extra resolution per edge is the reason to spend cluster time on this rather than on re-running a set-test with the same 13 genes.

**Concrete pipeline (mirrors the existing `04_rerconverge.R` machinery, which builds per-gene ML branch-length trees on the shared topology but has not yet been run to completion — no `report/rer_results.csv` exists yet):**
1. Build per-gene ML branch-length trees (phangorn, on the fixed primate topology) for all 77 panel genes from the existing codon alignments (`aln117_codon.tar.gz`, already extracted per-gene in `aln/`).
2. Compute each gene's relative evolutionary rate (RER) vector across shared branches (RERconverge's core transform).
3. Compute the full 77x77 pairwise rate-correlation (mirrortree/ERC) matrix.
4. Test whether the 15 (or 18) true within-panel GRN-edge pairs have significantly higher |correlation| than the remaining ~2,900 non-edge pairs among the panel, using the same degree-preserving-rewiring null as Tier 1 (applied now to the larger, still-sparse edge set).

**SLURM shape**, consistent with `comparative-genomics/README.md` conventions (account `tlasisi0`, partition `standard`, smoke-test-one-unit-first):
- Step 1 (per-gene tree building), array job: `--array=1-77`, one task per gene (same array pattern as `relax_array.sbatch`/`absrel_full_panel_array.sbatch`). Per-gene alignments in this panel run 36-91 tips (well under the full 304-tip master tree), so ML branch-length optimization on a few hundred codon columns is light: `--cpus-per-task=4 --mem=4G --time=00:30:00`. Smoke-test the single largest alignment first (by n_tips/n_sites in `fit_health.csv`) as a short job, read `seff`, then submit the full array.
- Step 2 (aggregation: RER transform + pairwise correlation matrix + permutation test), single task: small enough to run on the login-adjacent compute node or even locally afterward once the per-gene rate vectors are pulled back — `--cpus-per-task=1 --mem=8G --time=00:20:00`.
- Total wall-clock for the array: bounded by the smoke-test's `--time=00:30:00` cap, i.e. under an hour end-to-end once submitted, well inside a single short cluster session.

**What this buys over Tier 1:** if the network-topology-coordination signal is real but too subtle for a 13-node/15-edge K-comparison to detect, a richer per-pair statistic (whole-tree rate covariation) gives every one of those same 15-18 edges more chances to show it, without needing any new sequencing or alignment data — it is purely a better test of the SAME existing alignments and tree.

**Also queue (Tier-2-adjacent, cheap to add to the same submission): fill the remaining 11 of 14 independent origins** for per-origin RELAX (`per_origin_relax_array.sbatch` already exists and follows the same array pattern; ~1h/task). This is not required for the Tier-1/Tier-2 tests above, but is the natural next step if either shows a positive signal: repeating the topology test WITHIN each of the 14 independent origins and asking whether the coordination pattern recurs across independent origins is the convergence-based version of the claim, and is far more persuasive than a single pooled test — this project already treats independent-origin counting, not species counting, as the right measure of power (see `origin_assignments.csv`, 14 origins).

## Confounds and honesty notes (apply to both tiers)

- **Small within-panel edge count (15-18) is the dominant limitation**, not the statistical machinery. Both tiers are testing the same handful of true edges; Tier 2 improves the per-edge signal, it does not increase the edge count. A null result at either tier should be reported as "underpowered to detect anything but a large coordination effect," not as "no coordination exists."
- **Hub/pleiotropy dilution.** MITF and SOX10 are melanogenesis master regulators with broad pleiotropic roles beyond pigmentation; their pooled RELAX K may be diluted by non-pigmentation constraint exactly as this project's existing centrality-vs-constraint work (`internal/network-evo-explore/`) already found no simple centrality effect using human population-genetic constraint. A GRN-adjacency effect detected here would need to be distinguished from a generic "hub genes look different" artifact — the degree-preserving rewiring null partially controls for this (it preserves the degree sequence, including MITF's hub status, in every permutation), but does not control for expression breadth/pleiotropy directly, since we hold no cross-species expression data.
- **Network curation bias.** The 168-node core GRN was hand-curated around melanogenesis; genes absent from it (61 of 77 panel genes, essentially the entire hormone axis) are absent because they were never curated in, not because they lack real regulatory connections to the pigmentation genes. Any edge-level test is therefore scoped strictly to "the part of the pathway someone already drew a diagram of," not to the true underlying regulatory network.
- **aBSREL missingness is not random.** 6 of 13 connected genes have empty aBSREL JSONs; if these failures correlate with alignment quality (short/gappy alignments are exactly the ones aBSREL's stop-codon/frame cleaning step tends to fail on), any per-branch analysis built only from the 7 surviving genes would be biased toward better-behaved alignments, not toward any real biology.
